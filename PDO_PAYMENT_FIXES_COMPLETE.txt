ISSUES FIXED:

1. ✅ PAYMENT TRANSACTIONS - "VERIFY" BUTTON FOR AWAITING PAYMENT PDOs

   it shows as "Awaiting Payment" but still displays a generic "Verify" button.

   - Changed button text dynamically based on payment type and status
   - Updated tooltip to provide context-specific guidance

   Problem: Once a PDO is verified (status='verified'), it completely disappears 
   from the PDO Management tab. No way to view completed PDO transactions.
   
   Solution: Added third section "Verified" to PDO Management tab:
   - Tab 1: "Pending Approval" - PDOs awaiting admin approval
   - Tab 2: "Awaiting Payment" - Approved PDOs waiting to clear
   - Tab 3: "Verified" (NEW) - Completed/verified PDO payments
   
   File Modified: PDOTab.jsx
   Changes:
   - Added `verifiedPDOs` state to store verified PDO payments
   - Added `verifiedColumns` with appropriate columns for verified PDOs
   - Modified filtering logic to categorize verified PDOs separately
   - Added "Verified" tab button with count badge
   - Added verified section content with DataTable
   - Updated stats calculation to include verified count

   Question: "installment number? if pdo paano sa magkaka 10 or 20?"
   (How does installment numbering work for PDOs with 10 or 20 installments?)
   
   Answer: The system already handles any number of installments correctly:
   - Each installment has an `installment_number` field (integer: 1, 2, 3, ... 10, 20, etc.)
   - Display format: "#1", "#2", "#3", ... "#10", "#20", etc.
   - Full payments (non-installment): Shows "Full Payment" instead of number
   - PDO payments can be either:
     * Standalone PDO (full payment) - Shows "Full Payment"
     * Installment PDO (part of payment plan) - Shows "#1", "#2", etc.
   
   Display Logic:
   ```javascript
   {row.installment_number ? `#${row.installment_number}` : 'Full Payment'}
   ```
   
   Example with 10 installments:
   - Payment Plan: ₱10,000 total, split into 10 installments of ₱1,000 each
   - Shows in Payment Plans tab as "0/10 paid" initially
   - Each installment shows: "#1", "#2", "#3", ... "#10"
   - Customer pays installment #1 with PDO check
   - Shows in PDO Management under customer's name with "Installment: #1"
   - After all 10 paid: Shows "10/10 paid" with status "Completed"

PDO WORKFLOW - THREE STAGES:
============================

STAGE 1: PENDING APPROVAL
--------------------------
Status: status='pending', pdo_approval_status='pending' (or null)
Location: PDO Management → "Pending Approval" tab
Actions Available:
- ✅ Approve Check (CheckCircle button)
- ❌ Reject Check (XCircle button)
- 👁️ View Details (Eye button)

What Happens:
- Customer submits PDO check (image, check number, bank, date)
- Payment appears in "Pending Approval" section
- Admin reviews check authenticity
- Admin clicks "Approve" or "Reject"

STAGE 2: AWAITING PAYMENT
--------------------------
Status: status='pending', pdo_approval_status='approved'
Location: PDO Management → "Awaiting Payment" tab
Actions Available:
- ✅ Mark as Paid (CheckCircle button)
- 👁️ View Details (Eye button)

What Happens:
- Check has been approved by admin
- Waiting for check to clear at bank
- Payment shows as "Awaiting Payment" status
- Days/weeks later, admin confirms check cleared
- Admin clicks "Mark as Paid" button
- Payment moves to "Verified" section

STAGE 3: VERIFIED (COMPLETED)
------------------------------
Status: status='verified'
Location: PDO Management → "Verified" tab
Actions Available:
- 👁️ View Details (Eye button only)
- No modification buttons (read-only)

What Happens:
- Check has cleared successfully
- Payment is complete
- Appears in verified history
- Cannot be modified (final state)
- Visible for auditing and record-keeping


PAYMENT TRANSACTIONS TAB LOGIC:
===============================

Button Behavior Based on PDO State:
-----------------------------------

1. PDO PENDING APPROVAL
   Status: status='pending', pdo_approval_status='pending'
   Button: ❌ Verify (DISABLED)
   Tooltip: "PDO must be approved first in PDO Management tab"
   Reasoning: Must go through two-stage approval process

2. PDO AWAITING PAYMENT
   Status: status='pending', pdo_approval_status='approved'
   Button: ✅ Mark as Paid (ENABLED)
   Tooltip: "Mark PDO check as paid/cleared"
   Reasoning: Check is approved, ready to mark as cleared

3. REGULAR GCASH PAYMENT
   Status: status='needs_verification'
   Button: ✅ Verify (ENABLED)
   Tooltip: "Verify payment"
   Reasoning: Standard GCash verification process

4. ALREADY VERIFIED
   Status: status='verified'
   Button: ❌ Verify (DISABLED)
   Tooltip: "Cannot verify this payment"
   Reasoning: Already completed


INSTALLMENT PAYMENT PLAN DETAILS:
=================================

How Installments Work:
----------------------
1. Customer orders ₱10,000 worth of laundry services
2. Chooses installment payment: 5 installments
3. System creates payment plan:
   - Installment #1: ₱2,000 (due: May 5, 2026)
   - Installment #2: ₱2,000 (due: May 12, 2026)
   - Installment #3: ₱2,000 (due: May 19, 2026)
   - Installment #4: ₱2,000 (due: May 26, 2026)
   - Installment #5: ₱2,000 (due: June 2, 2026)

PDO Installment Payment Flow:
-----------------------------
1. Customer wants to pay Installment #1 with PDO check
2. Customer provides:
   - Check Number: 12345678
   - Bank: BDO
   - Check Date: May 10, 2026
   - Check Image: [uploads photo]
3. Payment appears in PDO Management → Pending Approval
   - Shows: "Installment: #1" in the table
4. Admin approves check
   - Moves to: PDO Management → Awaiting Payment
   - Still shows: "Installment: #1"
5. Check clears, admin marks as paid
   - Moves to: PDO Management → Verified
   - Still shows: "Installment: #1"
6. Payment Plans tab updates:
   - Changes from "0/5 paid" to "1/5 paid"
   - Installment #1 marked with green checkmark
   - Balance: ₱8,000 remaining

Multiple Installments with PDO:
-------------------------------
- Customer can pay EACH installment with PDO
- Each will appear separately in PDO Management
- Each has its own installment_number (#1, #2, #3, etc.)
- Each goes through the same two-stage approval process

Example with 20 Installments:
- Order: ₱100,000 split into 20 monthly payments of ₱5,000
- Customer pays all 20 via PDO checks
- PDO Management will show 20 separate entries:
  * "Installment: #1" through "Installment: #20"
- Each check is approved/verified independently
- Payment Plans shows: "0/20 paid" → "1/20 paid" → ... → "20/20 paid"
