================================================================================
  PAYMENT METHODS EXPLAINED
================================================================================

1. CASH
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Physical cash payment
Status Flow: Paid immediately (no verification needed)
Use Case: Walk-in customers, immediate payment
Admin/Customer: Both can use

Example:
  Order: ₱100,000
  Payment: ₱100,000 Cash
  Status: ✅ Paid (immediately)

2. GCASH
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: GCash mobile payment with proof
Status Flow: Paid → Needs Verification → Verified & Paid
Use Case: Digital payment, requires screenshot proof
Admin/Customer: Both can use

Example:
  Order: ₱100,000
  Payment: ₱100,000 GCash
  Status: ⏳ Paid - Needs Verification
  
  After admin verifies:
  Status: ✅ Verified & Paid

Verification Process:
  1. Customer pays via GCash
  2. Customer uploads proof (screenshot)
  3. System marks as "Paid - Needs Verification"
  4. Admin checks GCash account
  5. Admin clicks "Verify Payment"
  6. Status changes to "Verified & Paid"

3. PDO (POST-DATED ORDER)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Payment via post-dated check or bank commitment
Status Flow: Pending Approval → Approved → Awaiting Payment → Paid
Use Case: Corporate clients, large orders, credit terms
Admin/Customer: Both can use (with approval)

Example:
  Order: ₱100,000
  Payment: PDO Check #12345 (due May 15, 2026)
  Status: 🏦 Pending Approval
  
  After admin approves:
  Status: 🏦 Approved - Awaiting Payment (Due May 15)
  
  On May 15 when check clears:
  Status: ✅ Paid

4. PAY LATER (Better terms: "Credit" or "On Account")
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Customer will pay later, no specific date
Status Flow: Not Paid → Paid (when customer returns)
Use Case: Trusted customers, regular clients
Admin/Customer: Both can use

Better Term Options:
  - "Credit" (most common in business)
  - "On Account" (accounting term)
  - "Charge to Account"
  - "Bill Later"

Recommended: "Credit" or "On Account"

Example:
  Order: ₱100,000
  Payment: Credit (Pay Later)
  Status: ⚠️ Not Paid - On Account
  
  When customer returns and pays:
  Status: ✅ Paid

================================================================================
  STAGGERED PAYMENT OPTION
================================================================================

HOW IT WORKS:
-----------------------------------------------------------------------

At POS/Checkout, there's a checkbox:
  ☐ Enable Staggered Payment (Pay in Installments)

If UNCHECKED (Default - Full Payment):
  → Customer must pay full amount now
  → Choose ONE payment method
  → Order completed immediately (after verification if GCash)

If CHECKED (Staggered Payment):
  → Customer can pay in parts
  → Setup payment schedule
  → Each installment can use different method
  → Order completed when all installments paid

================================================================================
  PARTIAL PAYMENT (WITHOUT STAGGERED)
================================================================================

What if customer wants to pay PART now, rest later, but NO schedule?
-----------------------------------------------------------------------

Option 1: Use Staggered with 2 installments
  - Installment 1: ₱50,000 Cash [✓ Pay Now]
  - Installment 2: ₱50,000 Credit (pay anytime)

Option 2: Pay partial now, record rest later
  - Pay ₱50,000 Cash now
  - System creates automatic 2nd installment: ₱50,000 Credit
  - Customer pays remaining when they return

Recommended: Option 1 (explicit staggered)
  - Clearer for tracking
  - Customer knows they have balance
  - Admin can see payment structure

Example:
  Customer: "I'll pay ₱50k now, rest later"
  
  Staff:
    1. ☑ Enable Staggered Payment
    2. Add 2 installments:
       - Installment 1: ₱50,000 Cash [✓ Pay Now]
       - Installment 2: ₱50,000 Credit (no due date)
    3. Save schedule
  
  Result:
    - Order Status: Partial Payment
    - Paid: ₱50,000
    - Balance: ₱50,000
    - Customer can pay balance anytime

================================================================================
  PAYMENT STATUS FLOW
================================================================================

CASH PAYMENT:
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Status Flow: Paid ✅ (immediately verified)

Example:
  Customer pays ₱100,000 Cash
  → Status: ✅ Paid
  → No verification needed
  → Order can be fulfilled immediately

GCASH PAYMENT:
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Status Flow: Paid - Needs Verification ⏳ → Verified & Paid ✅

Example:
  Customer pays ₱100,000 GCash
  → Status: ⏳ Paid - Needs Verification
  → Admin checks GCash account
  → Admin clicks "Verify Payment"
  → Status: ✅ Verified & Paid
  → Order can be fulfilled

Database:
  payment_installments.status = 'needs_verification'
  payments.status = 'needs_verification'
  
  After verification:
  payment_installments.status = 'verified'
  payments.status = 'verified'
  sales.amount_paid += amount
  sales.balance_remaining -= amount

PDO PAYMENT:
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Status Flow: Pending Approval 🏦 → Approved 🏦 → Awaiting Payment 🏦 → Paid ✅

Example:
  Customer provides PDO check ₱100,000 (due May 15)
  → Status: 🏦 Pending Approval
  → Admin reviews check + customer credit
  → Admin clicks "Approve PDO"
  → Status: 🏦 Approved - Awaiting Payment (Due May 15)
  → On May 15, check clears
  → Admin clicks "Mark as Paid"
  → Status: ✅ Paid
  → Order fulfilled

Database:
  payment_installments.status = 'pending'
  payment_installments.pdo_approval_status = 'pending'
  
  After approval:
  payment_installments.pdo_approval_status = 'approved'
  
  After payment received:
  payment_installments.status = 'paid'
  Create payment record
  sales.amount_paid += amount
  sales.balance_remaining -= amount

CREDIT (PAY LATER) PAYMENT:
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Status Flow: Not Paid ⚠️ → Paid ✅ (when customer returns)

Example:
  Customer orders ₱100,000 on Credit
  → Status: ⚠️ Not Paid - On Account
  → Customer returns later
  → Customer pays (Cash or GCash)
  → Status: ✅ Paid

Database:
  payment_installments.status = 'pending'
  payment_installments.payment_method = 'credit'
  
  When customer pays:
  Create payment record (actual method used)
  payment_installments.status = 'paid'
  sales.amount_paid += amount
  sales.balance_remaining -= amount
