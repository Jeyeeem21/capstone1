NEW CONCEPT: "PAYMENT SCHEDULE" (Not "Payment Method")
-----------------------------------------------------------------------

Key Insight:
  - Don't ask "What's the payment method?"
  - Ask "What's the payment schedule?"
  
  Payment Schedule = Timeline of all planned payments
  Each payment in the schedule can use ANY method

REDESIGNED APPROACH:
-----------------------------------------------------------------------

At POS, instead of selecting ONE payment method, you create a
PAYMENT SCHEDULE with multiple payment entries.

Example:
  Order: ₱100,000
  
  Payment Schedule:
    Entry 1: ₱20,000 - PDO (check #123, due May 15)
    Entry 2: ₱20,000 - Cash (pay now)
    Entry 3: ₱10,000 - GCash (pay now)
    Entry 4: ₱30,000 - PDO (check #456, due June 15)
    Entry 5: ₱20,000 - Cash (pay later, no due date)
  
  Total: ₱100,000 ✓

This is essentially a FLEXIBLE STAGGERED PLAN where:
  - Each entry can use different method
  - Some entries have due dates (PDO)
  - Some entries are immediate (Cash/GCash now)
  - Some entries are flexible (Cash/GCash later)

NEW TERMINOLOGY:
-----------------------------------------------------------------------

OLD (Confusing):
  - Payment Method: cash, gcash, cod, pay_later
  - Payment Type: partial, staggered, pdo
  
NEW (Clear):
  - Payment Entry Method: cash, gcash, pdo
  - Payment Schedule Type: 
    • simple (one payment, full amount)
    • flexible (multiple payments, any method, any timing)
    • structured (multiple payments, fixed schedule)

REMOVE THESE CONCEPTS:
  ❌ "Payment Method" at order level (too limiting)
  ❌ "COD" as payment method (it's just cash paid later)
  ❌ "Pay Later" as payment method (it's just deferred payment)
  ❌ Mutual exclusivity between partial/staggered/pdo

KEEP THESE CONCEPTS:
  ✅ Payment Schedule (list of all planned payments)
  ✅ Payment Entry (individual payment with method + amount + timing)
  ✅ Payment Status (paid, partial, not_paid)

SIMPLIFIED STRUCTURE:

Q4: "So basically if the payment is not complete it will be staggered? 
     Or iba pa yung staggered na option for it?"
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
A: Great question! Let me clarify:

STAGGERED is now just ONE TYPE of payment schedule!

Payment Schedule Types:
  
  1. SIMPLE (one payment, full amount)
     - Customer pays full ₱100,000 now
     - One entry, one method
     - Example: ₱100,000 Cash (pay now)
  
  2. FLEXIBLE (multiple payments, any method, any timing)
     - Your scenarios! Mix of cash/gcash/pdo
     - No fixed schedule, customer decides when
     - Example: ₱50k now, ₱30k later, ₱20k PDO
  
  3. STRUCTURED (traditional staggered - fixed schedule)
     - Fixed installments with due dates
     - Equal amounts, regular intervals
     - Example: ₱20k down + 4 x ₱20k/month
     - Can still use different methods per installment!

So:
  - Incomplete payment ≠ automatically staggered
  - Incomplete payment = flexible schedule
  - Staggered = structured schedule with fixed dates

Example Comparison:

  FLEXIBLE Schedule:
    Entry 1: ₱50,000 Cash (pay now)
    Entry 2: ₱30,000 GCash (pay whenever)
    Entry 3: ₱20,000 PDO (due May 15)
    → No fixed pattern, customer flexibility
  
  STRUCTURED Schedule (Traditional Staggered):
    Entry 1: ₱20,000 Cash (down payment, pay now)
    Entry 2: ₱20,000 Any method (due May 15)
    Entry 3: ₱20,000 Any method (due Jun 15)
    Entry 4: ₱20,000 Any method (due Jul 15)
    Entry 5: ₱20,000 Any method (due Aug 15)
    → Fixed pattern, scheduled dates, late fees apply

Both use the SAME database structure!
Just different schedule_type and due_date patterns.

================================================================================
  REMOVING COD AND PAY_LATER AS "PAYMENT METHODS"
================================================================================

CURRENT PROBLEM:
  - COD (Cash on Delivery) is confusing
  - Pay Later is redundant
  - They're not really "methods", they're "timing"

NEW APPROACH:
-----------------------------------------------------------------------

Remove these as payment methods:
  ❌ COD
  ❌ Pay Later

Keep only ACTUAL payment methods:
  ✅ Cash
  ✅ GCash
  ✅ PDO (Post-Dated Order)

How to handle COD and Pay Later:

  OLD: "Payment Method: COD"
  NEW: "Payment Schedule: Flexible"
       Entry 1: Cash, amount=₱100,000, due_date=null, status='pending'
       → This IS "pay later" - cash payment with no due date

  OLD: "Payment Method: Pay Later"
  NEW: Same as above - just a pending cash payment

  OLD: "Payment Method: COD" (cash on delivery)
  NEW: "Payment Schedule: Flexible"
       Entry 1: Cash, amount=₱100,000, due_date=[delivery_date]
       → Cash payment due on delivery date

Benefits:
  - Simpler system (3 methods instead of 5)
  - More flexible (can mix timing with any method)
  - Clearer meaning (method = how you pay, not when)

Example Conversions:

  OLD System:
    Order 1: Payment Method = "COD"
    Order 2: Payment Method = "Pay Later"
    Order 3: Payment Method = "Cash"
  
  NEW System:
    Order 1: Schedule Type = "Flexible"
             Entry 1: Cash, ₱100k, due_date=[delivery], status='pending'
    
    Order 2: Schedule Type = "Flexible"
             Entry 1: Cash, ₱100k, due_date=null, status='pending'
    
    Order 3: Schedule Type = "Simple"
             Entry 1: Cash, ₱100k, due_date=null, status='paid'

All three use "Cash" as method, but different timing/status!

  MIGRATION STRATEGY: OLD SYSTEM → NEW SYSTEM
================================================================================

For existing orders in old system:
-----------------------------------------------------------------------

1. Orders with payment_method = 'cash' and payment_status = 'paid':
   → Create simple schedule
   → One entry: cash, full amount, status='paid'

2. Orders with payment_method = 'gcash' and payment_status = 'paid':
   → Create simple schedule
   → One entry: gcash, full amount, status='paid'

3. Orders with payment_method = 'cod' and payment_status = 'not_paid':
   → Create flexible schedule
   → One entry: cash, full amount, due_date=[delivery], status='pending'

4. Orders with payment_method = 'pay_later' and payment_status = 'not_paid':
   → Create flexible schedule
   → One entry: cash, full amount, due_date=null, status='pending'

5. Orders with existing payment_plan (staggered):
   → Create structured schedule
   → Convert each installment to schedule entry
   → Preserve due dates and amounts

6. Orders with existing post_dated_order (PDO):
   → Create flexible schedule
   → Entry 1: down payment (if any)
   → Entry 2: pdo, remaining amount, due_date=[pdo_date]
